Explore our local wonders

Mt. Threefingers

Just as you reach the peak of Smokey Point's freeway overpass, the majestic Mt. Threefingers stands as a grand welcome to the town.

Kayak Point

A popular beach spot just a short drive from the Smokey Point area. Nearby Camano Island protects this spot from ocean waves, giving this saltwater beach a lake-ish feel.

Arlington Airport

This private local airport is home to not only a scenic view but also countless privately owned small planes that are a wonder to see in action.

Marysville Wetlands

Just south lies a beautiful wetland area, home to walkways and waterways where you'll find bikers, boaters and kayakers alike!

Lakewood High School

This school is the home and gathering place to many here. It hosts huge extracurricular events, such as a cross-country meet that brings in teams from the surrounding states.

Centennial Trail

Just east of the town you can find the scenic centennial trail, which is home to walkers and bikers and weaves around farms, rivers, and lakes. A historic barn marks the north end of this trail.

Stillaguamish River

This scenic, lazy-flowing river winds its way through and around the area, and is an excellent spot for fishing, kayaking, swimming, and tubing.

Silvana

With a population of 94, this tiny historic town is just minutes outside the Smokey Point area. It's main street is about a block long and home to a family restaurant, a five and dime, and a butcher shop.

Lake Goodwin

This is one of seven lakes west of Smokey point that make up the "seven lakes" area. Each is a great spot for fishing and has plenty of parks and public access points.
